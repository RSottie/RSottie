## About Me

I am a computational nanophotonics researcher specializing in the optical properties 
of plasmonic nanostructures and machine learning-driven spectral prediction. My PhD 
research combines electromagnetic simulations (COMSOL) with deep learning frameworks 
to solve forward and inverse design problems in nanophotonics.

---

## Research Interests
- Plasmonics and optical chirality in gold nanostructures
- Weakly supervised learning for spectral prediction
- Forward and inverse design of nanoparticle optical properties
- Multipole resonances in gold nanorods
- Computational electromagnetics (COMSOL)
- X-ray scanning tunneling microscopy

## Projects

### 🔬 Weakly Supervised Learning for Plasmonic Spectra
- Forward and inverse neural network models for predicting extinction spectra of gold nanospheres
- Heteroscedastic noise-aware loss function (Kendall & Gal NLL)
- Comparison of FSL vs WSL across 5 noise levels
- **Tools:** Python, TensorFlow, NumPy, Matplotlib

### 🔬 Chiroptical Responses in Gold Nanorod Dimers
- COMSOL simulations of plasmonic chirality in asymmetric Au nanorod assemblies
- Multipole decomposition of optical responses
- **Tools:** COMSOL Multiphysics, Mathematica, Python

### 🔬 Multipole Resonances in Gold Nanorods
- Published study on optical sensitivity of multipole resonant Au nanorods
- **Tools:** COMSOL, Python
